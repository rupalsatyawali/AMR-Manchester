Anti Microbial Resistance Poster

Antimicrobial Resistance (AMR) occurs when bacteria evolve and adapt so that antibiotics no longer work against them. This usually happens because of overuse or misuse of antibiotics in medicine and agriculture. When bacteria survive repeated exposure to these drugs, they become harder to treat, leading to longer illnesses, increased healthcare costs, and higher mortality. Understanding AMR is essential for preventing the spread of resistant strains and ensuring that antibiotics remain effective for future generations.
